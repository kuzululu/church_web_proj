New community center envisioned to bring congregation closer together

The Sacred Heart Shrine of Jesus Parish is an religious organization that serves as a vital part of the communal and spiritual lives of its members. The parish lays plans for many events carried out such as Masses, religious ceremonies, fundraisers, community outreach initiatives, and various social gatherings.

Established in 1941, the Sacred Heart of Jesus Parish is the first church to serve both the residents of the government’s Homesite Barrio Obrero and the wealth residents of the nearby Magdalena Estate. Before that, the residents in both area attended masses at the chapels of the Convent of Carmel of Thérèse of Lisieux

(Est. 1932), and the Christ the King Mission Seminary (Est. 1933); which are all located within the Magdalena Estates. Upon the 1939 establishment of the Homesite Project’s Barrio Obrero, the residents approached the missionaries of the Society of the Divine Word (SVD, or Societas Verbi Divini) from the nearby Divine Word Mission Seminary, to hold masses in their neighborhood. So Christmas day 1940, Fr. Herman Kondring, of the SDV, gave the first mass in a makeshift chapel along K-A Street (now Vice-Mayor Luis Sianghio Street); which now the site of the Kamuning Public Market. Around 100 parishioners attended that first mass, from the 500 families who had settled in the Kamuning area.
